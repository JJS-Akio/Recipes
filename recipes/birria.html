Birria

Birria is a rich and aromatic Mexican stew, traditionally made with slow-cooked goat or beef, soaked in a robust and spicy adobo sauce made from a blend of dried chiles, garlic, and herbs. This savory dish not only warms the soul with its deep flavors but also offers a festive, communal eating experience, perfect for gatherings. Simple to prepare yet brimming with complex flavors, birria promises a culinary adventure that makes it worth every minute spent cooking, inviting you to dive into a beloved staple of Mexican cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Beef, goat, or lamb meat (about 3-4 pounds)
  • Dried chiles (such as guajillo, ancho, or pasilla)
  • Garlic cloves
  • Onion
  • Tomatoes or tomato sauce
  • Cumin, oregano, cloves, and other spices
  • Bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Water or broth
  • Optional: cilantro, lime wedges, and chopped onions for garnish
  • Optional: corn tortillas for serving

Instructions

  1. Toast the dried chiles in a dry skillet until fragrant, then soak them in hot water until they are soft.
  2. In a blender, combine the softened chiles, garlic, onion, tomatoes or tomato sauce, and spices to make the marinade.
  3. Season the meat with salt and pepper and then coat it thoroughly with the marinade. Let it marinate for several hours or overnight in the refrigerator.
  4. In a large pot, place the marinated meat and cover with water or broth. Add bay leaves.
  5. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 3-4 hours or until the meat is tender and falls apart easily.
  6. Shred the meat with forks and return it to the pot. Adjust seasoning as needed.
  7. Serve the birria in bowls with plenty of broth, garnished with cilantro, lime, and onions. Accompany with warm corn tortillas.

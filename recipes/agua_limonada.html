Mexican Lemonade

Mexican lemonade, also known as "agua de limonada," is a refreshing blend of freshly squeezed lime juice, pure water, and a touch of sweetness, often elevated with a hint of mint. This vibrant drink is not only thirst-quenching but also serves as a perfect complement to any meal, especially during warm weather, enhancing your dining experience with its zesty and invigorating flavors. Simple to prepare and universally loved, this lemonade offers a delightful taste of Mexican cuisine that can bring a festive touch to your gatherings, making every sip a celebration of freshness

Ingredients

  • Fresh limes (about 6-8, depending on size and desired tartness)
  • Water (about 6 cups)
  • Sugar, honey, or another sweetener to taste (typically about 1/2 cup)
  • Ice cubes
  • Optional: fresh mint leaves for garnish and added flavor

Instructions

  1. Thoroughly wash the limes and cut them in half.
  2. Squeeze the juice from the limes into a pitcher, using a juicer or by hand.
  3. Add the sugar or sweetener to the lime juice and stir until fully dissolved.
  4. Pour the water into the pitcher and mix well with the lime juice and sugar mixture.
  5. Taste and adjust sweetness or lime flavor according to preference.
  6. Add ice cubes to the pitcher or to individual glasses when serving.
  7. Garnish with fresh mint leaves, if using, to enhance the flavor.
  8. Serve chilled and enjoy the refreshing taste!
